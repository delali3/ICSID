Submission Guidelines

Complete guidelines for manuscript preparation and submission

1

Paper Templates

Authors must use the official IEEE conference template. Templates are available in Microsoft Word and LaTeX formats.

Download the template from the IEEE website before beginning manuscript preparation.

Guidance text in the template must be removed before submission.

Download Templates:

  • LaTeX Template (ZIP)
  • Microsoft Word Template (A4)
2

Manuscript Formatting

Manuscript should be in double-column format.

Use 10 pt font size, unless the template specifies otherwise.

Margins should follow IEEE standard (usually ~ top 0.75", bottom 1", side margins 0.625", or corresponding metric/A4 size equivalents).

Do not add page numbers, headers, or footers (these will be added later by the publisher).

All figures and tables should be embedded within the text, with clear labels. Use vector graphics (EPS, PDF) whenever possible. If using bitmaps (JPEG, PNG), ensure a sufficient resolution (≥150 dpi) and check legibility in print in black and white.

3

Length

Papers should be between 6 and 10 pages in length, including figures, tables, and references.

4

Submission of the Final (Camera-Ready) Version

After acceptance, authors must submit a camera-ready version using the IEEE template, which has been fully revised in accordance with the reviewers' comments.

Authors must register for the conference.

5

IEEE PDF eXpress & PDF Compliance

Authors must use IEEE PDF eXpress to verify that their PDF is IEEE Xplore-compatible.

Use the correct Conference ID (#69485)

After conversion/checking, download the approved/generated PDF and submit it to the conference organisers.

6

Copyright / Electronic Forms

Authors must complete and submit the electronic IEEE Copyright Form (eCF) prior to publication.

7

Presentation Requirement and No-Show Policy

At least one author of each accepted paper must register and present the paper.

If a paper is not presented (or by a co-author), it may be excluded from proceedings and IEEE Xplore.

8

Review and Revision

Following acceptance, authors may be asked to address reviewers' comments. The final version should incorporate these changes.

Check for typographical errors, layout issues, and ensure consistency (fonts, figure formatting, references).
